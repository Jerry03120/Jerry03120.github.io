I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow in the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering at the University of Houston, working with Dr. Abigail L. Beck. My research focuses on applying machine learning and computer vision to improve the resilience of critical infrastructure — particularly power grids and coastal utility systems — against extreme weather events. I completed my Ph.D. in Civil Engineering at Texas A&M University under Dr. Stephanie German Paal, and interned at Oak Ridge National Laboratory as part of the Grid Resilience Research Group.

Research Interests

  • ML for Critical Infrastructure Resilience
  • Power Grid Analytics & Forecasting
  • Explainable AI for Decision Support
  • LiDAR & Machine Vision
  • Community Resilience & Big Data
  • LLMs for Emergency Planning

Research Experience

2025–present
Postdoctoral Research Fellow
University of Houston · PI: Dr. Abigail L. Beck
  • Building data-driven frameworks integrating 311 service requests and socio-demographic data to quantify urban resilience across Houston neighborhoods.
  • Implementing Grounded SAM for automated utility pole deterioration assessment via LiDAR point cloud integration.
  • Investigating U.S. coastal infrastructure quality through statistical modeling and fieldwork validation.
2022–2025
Doctoral Researcher
Texas A&M University · Advisor: Dr. Stephanie G. Paal
  • Developed ML frameworks for large-scale power outage prediction across nine U.S. states using multi-source meteorological and socio-demographic datasets.
  • Optimized ensemble models (XGBoost, LightGBM, CatBoost) with Bayesian hyperparameter tuning and SHAP-based explainability.
  • Deployed near-real-time prediction systems; applied transfer learning for data-sparse climate hazard scenarios.
May–Aug 2024
Research Intern

  • Contributed to one journal article (IEEE Access) and one conference paper (IEEE BigData 2024).
2016–2021
Graduate Research Assistant
